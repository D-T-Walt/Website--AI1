    Summary and Reflection

      The “Twelve Pillars” by Jim Rohn and Chris Widner is a motivational book that guides its readers towards self-improvement by blending storytelling and advice. The story does so by following
    12 “pillars” that represent essential principles involved in personal growth and achievement. The story revolves around Michael Jones who encounters a much older man, and caretaker of the Twelve
    Pillars estate, by the name of Charlie who through their many conversations relates to Michael, various life lessons. We will be delving into a select number of these life lessons that were imparted.

    Chapter Four

      In Chapter 4 of the book, Charlie communicates the importance of setting goals. This includes the various types that exist.

    Long Term
    Accomplishments to be made in the future 5+ years away
    Mid-Range Term
    Accomplishments to by the half point of the long-term goal
    Short Term
    Accomplishments to be made 2- 3 years away
    Micro Goals
    Each goal is only months away
    All of which are in the effort to reach the final goal. One important point that was brought up was that goals set aren't only important because it is what a person wants to accomplish but rather goals
    play a part in personal development because of what a person would need to do and/ or endure to make sure they make their goal a reality. The concept of micro goals is a new concept to me that I
    can now utilize to make myself better and more equipped to tackle my long-term goals for my career. Also, with the motivational viewpoint that allows me to consider challenges faced in achieving
    my goal as tools in my personal development, I will be more encouraged to tackle all obstacles.

    Chapter Five

      In Chapter 5 of the book, Charlie communicates the importance of time management when Charlie starts to complain about not having time to set and achieve his goals. He highlights the fact
    that time is precious and how it is used greatly affects the degree of success a person gets in their endeavours. As a procrastinator I stand to gain a lot out of this chapter, it forces me to evaluate the
    way I make use of time while doing assignments. A point made that I will have to keep in mind going forward is that it is not always wise to attend to the most urgent matter but rather the most
    important.

    Chapter Six

      In Chapter 6 of the book, Charlie communicates the importance of the social circle around you. This is because your social circle greatly influences how you are. If an individual has persons
    around them that aren't very ambitious they will feel comfortable not accomplishing anything in that group because they would fit in. However, if the persons they surround themselves with are
    committed to personal growth and success to keep up with the group the individual will be prompted to also improve to also succeed. Charlie highlights the point that surrounding yourself with the
    right people also proves to build a network of accomplished personnel. The main part of this chapter that is of great help to me is his “rubric” to evaluate my group, the questions I need to ask myself
    about my social circle to determine if they are right for my personal growth.

    Chapter Seven

      In Chapter 7 of the book, Charlie illustrates the importance of being always willing to learn throughout life. Things, people and times change so there is always knowledge to be gained in the
    world. Charlie stressed the importance of books in gaining knowledge. This message especially applies to my career choice it is an always adapting sector that prompts its participants to always be
    gaining knowledge and learning new things so this ideology of always learning through life is something I will need to adopt in my life after university. However, until then, I will be committed to reading
    more non-fiction books.

      To conclude I am compelled to leave you with a profound quote from the book “A successful person is always a life-long learner.” If you wish to read “Twelve Pillars” for yourself click here.

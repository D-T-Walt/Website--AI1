    Resumé





    Professional Vision & Mission Statement

      It has always been an opinion of mine that it is important to have a plan to achieve a future/ outcome that I desire. The shaping of my professional path in software engineering is no different.
    Defining a clear vision and mission gives me something to focus on and keeps me motivated to perpetually improve. However it is important to understand the difference between the two.

    Vision Statement
    Illustrates my aspirations for the future, the ultimate point of my career where I have achieved all I want professionally.
    Mission Statement
    My present actions that I am using to achieve my vision, describing my current purpose and commitment.


    Vision Statement

      I aim to

    • Become a Senior Software Engineer at a major corporation or firm of my own, delivering high-quality, influential software solutions.
    • Master cutting-edge technologies and stay informed on trends in the industry to remain competitive.
    • Adapt to the ever-evolving technological field, ensuring continuous personal and professional growth.
    • Uphold excellence and quality in all aspects of software engineering, creating a permanent impact on the field, and Jamaica's technological infrastructure.


    Mission Statement

      Currently, I am in pursuit of a Bachelor of Science in Computing, my mission is to have a sturdy foundation in software development and computing principles. I am committed to applying my
    skills and knowledge to real-world projects creating robust and user-friendly software. I aim to grow and excel towards my goal of being a software engineer by taking advantage of learning opportunities
    and adopting new technologies. Laying this groundwork is essential to even more long-term growth, pursuing more advanced studies through a master's degree. All in an effort to excel in this
    always-changing technological space.


    My Career Choice

      As is the case for every job and/or career, a career in software engineering has skills, job requirements and qualifications to fulfil although these concepts are similar in some regards there are indeed
    differences.

    Skills
    The abilities, learnt through education and/or practice, needed to complete tasks in a professional setting
    Job Requirements
    These are tasks and resposibilities given within a role. This is synonymous to a job description
    Qualifications
    These are requirements a candidate have to meet, to be elgible for a position

    Skills
    1. Experience, or at least a working familiarity, with JavaScript, Linux/Unix, Java, Ruby, PHP, Python, Microsoft ASP.NET MVC, C, C++, and C#.
    2. Familiarity with UI Toolkits and frameworks.
    3. Ability to transform requirements into design concepts and entity relationship diagrams (ERDs).
    4. Ability to analyze complex technical information.
    5. Understanding of software engineering best practices.
    6. Written and verbal communication.
    7. Collaboration.
    8. Problem Solving
    9. Critical Thinking
    10. Time Management

    Job Requirements
    1. Modifying software to fix errors, adapt it to new hardware, improve its performance, or upgrade interfaces.
    2. Directing system testing and validation procedures.
    3. Directing software programming and documentation development.
    4. Working with customers or departments on technical issues including software system design and maintenance.
    5. Analyzing information to recommend and plan the installation of new systems or modifications of an existing system.
    6. Designing and developing software systems using scientific analysis and mathematical models to predict and measure outcomes and design consequences.
    7. Preparing reports on programming project specifications, activities, or status.
    8. Conferring with project managers to obtain information on limitations or capabilities.

    Qualifications
    1. Bachelor's Degree in Computing/ Software Engineering.
    2. Software Engineering Skills- Which is to be expected with a Bachelor's Degree in Computing.
    3. Internships- Gain hands-on experience and develop additional skills.
    4. Certifications- This is a very important component because the field is always growing.
    5. Master's Degree in Software Engineering or a Similar Field (for more senior positions/ specialised fields).

    Traits, Emotions and Hobbies Impact on my Academic Life

    Trait

      My most dominant personality trait is my nonchalant/laid-back nature. This trait allows me to handle academic pressure incredibly well,
    I don't get stressed about deadlines or get overwhelmed by difficult assignments, and this maintains a composed demeanour in situations
    where others may struggle and get stressed. Even so, being laid back has its disadvantages. At times, because I underestimate how much time
    I need to complete tasks, I procrastinate and do assignments at the last minute. Recently, I have had to balance working consistently and
    staying relaxed.

    Emotion

      Even though I am laid back my strongest emotion is still my anxiety, and it plays a significant role in my academic life, especially during
    assignments. I often times second-guess the work that I produce, worrying about whether I meet the expectations of my teachers. While this
    enables me to sometimes pay very close attention to details it also causes me to overthink tasks that should be simple and/or delay the starting
    of a project because I fear it won't be done properly.








    Hobby

      The watching of TV shows and sometimes movies has always been my premier choice for relaxation and leisure in and outside of my
    academic life. Especially during exam periods, allowing myself to get captivated by these fictional worlds, plots and characters proves very
    effective in giving my mind a break from studying. It is also important to note that when the plot is compelling, with several plot twists, for
    example in murder mysteries, it does wonders in improving my analytical skills and critical thinking which are essential to developing software
    solutions. However, as I am prone to procrastination, indulging in this pastime can become hazardous as binging shows are a welcome distraction
    during these periods.






    Hero


      My hero is Marcus Mosiah Garvey, he was Jamaica's first National Hero. He was born in Kingston, Jamaica, however throughout his career he travelled extensively, primarily the US and
    the UK. This gave him exposure to the poor working and living conditions of black people around the world. This led to him becoming an activist for black people worldwide. In 1914, he started
    the Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA), in Jamaica but it eventually grew internationally encouraging black self-governance for black people worldwide, self-help economic projects
    and protest against racial discrimination. It is for this reason why I chose him to be my hero, I consider my racial identity to be a very important part of my life and Marcus Garvey beliefs and
    ideologies bears alot of direct and indirect responsibility for the upliftment of black people today, because of his inciting of black pride. From the labour riots of the 1930s in the Caribbean to the
    American Civil Rights Movement many positive changes for black people were brought forth because Marcus Garvey chose to stand for what he believed in, which is a good trait for any individual
    to have. The quote I chose not only speaks to him standing up for black people but he is also explains to his people that it is important to know our history.


    Quote
    A people without the knowledge of their past history, origin, and culture is like a tree without roots.
    — Marcus Garvey

    Validity Reflection

      I chose to do the Keirsey personality test however I would say that the test is invalid because it uses a binary response system. There is no opportunity to give more nuanced resonses to the
    questions that were given. The results that I recieved, as seen above, hightligts this invalidity through points made about my personality that aren't true. The test concluded that I am a Artisan.
    It stated that artisans are optimistic, focused, trusting of their impulses and naturally gifted in the arts. This evaluation is wrong on all counts:

    • Optimistic:
      • I am a very pessimistic person, I tend to always expect the worst outcome out of a situation

    • Focused:
      • Generally, to remain engaged in an activity I need to be doing something else i.e listening to music, watching something etc.

    • Trusting of Impulses:
      • I constantly second guess myself.

    • Naturally gifted in the Arts:
      • I am not an artistic person, I find it very difficult to be creative
      In essence, I consider the test to be very invalid however would give more accurate and valid results if it gave a range of options similar to the Briggs Myers test.
